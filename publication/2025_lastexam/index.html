Humanity's Last Exam: A Multi-Modal Benchmark at the Frontier of Human Knowledge

Omid Taheri, & Many Others
April, 2025
Cite PDF arXiv Data Code SEAL LLM Leaderboards
Benchmark difficulty compared to existing academic tests
Type
1
Publication
arXiv preprint arXiv:2501.14249

Abstract

Benchmarks are essential for tracking rapid LLM progress—but today’s models exceed 90% on tasks like MMLU, saturating existing exams.
We introduce Humanity’s Last Exam (HLE), a multi-modal, closed-ended benchmark spanning 2,500 questions across 100+ subjects at the frontier of human knowledge.
All questions are publicly released; a held-out private test set prevents overfitting.

Teaser

Even the strongest frontier LLMs peak at ~21.6% accuracy on HLE, leaving ample headroom for progress.

Benchmark Design

  1. Question Collection: Curated 2,500 closed-ended items covering STEM, humanities, arts, and professional domains.
  2. Multi-Modal Inputs: Includes diagrams, charts, and short passages—tests both visual and textual reasoning.
  3. Evaluation Protocol: Public training/dev split; private test set for leaderboard submissions to guard against over-tuning.

Quantitative Results

Accuracy. State-of-the-art LLMs score between 2.7% and 21.6% on HLE.

Calibration Error. We also prompt each model for a confidence score (0–100%) to quantify over-confidence in wrong answers.

Leaderboards & Comparisons

In addition to our private test, HLE is live on SEAL’s public leaderboard.
Compared to MMLU (⟨90%⟩), ARC, and other academic exams, HLE remains unsolved—providing a durable benchmark.

Discussion

Current frontier models score under 22% and exhibit high calibration errors, underscoring both capability and confidence gaps.
Historically, benchmarks saturate rapidly—models could surpass 50% on HLE by late 2025, but expert-level, multi-modal closed-ended reasoning will remain a challenge beyond that point.
